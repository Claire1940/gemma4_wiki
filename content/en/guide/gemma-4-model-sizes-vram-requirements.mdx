## The Gemma 4 Model Family: Architecture and Efficiency

The Gemma 4 lineup is categorized into four primary tiers, each serving a specific niche from mobile efficiency to flagship-level performance. Unlike previous generations, Google has utilized a mix of dense and Mixture of Experts (MoE) architectures to maximize throughput while minimizing the active parameter count during inference.

| Model Variant | Architecture | Context Window | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gemma 4 2B** | Ultra-efficient Dense | 128K | Mobile and Edge devices |
| **Gemma 4 4B** | Multimodal Dense | 128K | Edge performance & vision tasks |
| **Gemma 4 26B** | Mixture of Experts (MoE) | 256K | High-speed local desktop agents |
| **Gemma 4 31B** | Flagship Dense | 256K | Top-tier reasoning & coding |

The 26B variant is particularly noteworthy for its efficiency; while it possesses 26 billion total parameters, it only activates approximately 4 billion during inference. This allows it to run at incredible speeds—up to 300 tokens per second on hardware like the Mac Studio M2 Ultra—making it one of the fastest models in its class for 2026.

## Gemma 4 Model Sizes VRAM Requirements for Local Setup

When running these models locally, the primary bottleneck is Video RAM (VRAM). The amount of memory required depends heavily on the quantization level (the precision of the model weights). While FP16 (full precision) offers the highest quality, most users in 2026 utilize 4-bit (Q4) or 8-bit (Q8) quantizations to fit larger models on consumer GPUs like the RTX 50-series or 60-series.

### Estimated VRAM Requirements by Quantization

| Model Size | 4-bit (Q4_K_M) | 8-bit (Q8_0) | FP16 (Uncompressed) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2B Variant** | ~1.8 GB | ~2.5 GB | ~5.0 GB |
| **4B Variant** | ~3.2 GB | ~4.8 GB | ~9.0 GB |
| **26B (MoE)** | ~16.5 GB | ~28.5 GB | ~52.0 GB |
| **31B (Dense)** | ~19.0 GB | ~33.0 GB | ~65.0 GB |

> **💡 Tip:** If you have exactly 16GB of VRAM, the 26B MoE model at Q4 quantization is your best bet for a balance of high intelligence and fluid speed. For 24GB VRAM cards (like the 3090/4090/5090), the 31B model at Q4 or Q5 fits comfortably with room for a large context window.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the minimum gemma 4 model sizes vram requirements for a playable experience?**

A: For a smooth experience, the 2B model requires as little as 2GB of VRAM at 4-bit quantization. However, for advanced tasks like coding or complex reasoning, the 26B MoE model is recommended, requiring at least 16GB of VRAM to run effectively.

**Q: Is Gemma 4 better for gaming logic than previous versions?**

A: Yes, Gemma 4 shows a massive leap in spatial reasoning and physics simulation. It can generate functional game rules, state management, and smooth motion mechanics in raw browser code much more reliably than Gemma 2 or 3.

**Q: Can I run the 31B model on a standard 8GB GPU?**

A: No, the 31B model is too large for an 8GB VRAM card, even at heavy quantization. You would need to use system RAM (GGUF format via llama.cpp), which will significantly slow down the tokens-per-second to a crawl.

**Q: Does Gemma 4 support languages other than English?**

A: Yes, the models support over 140 languages and feature an expanded context window of up to 256K, making them excellent for long-form translation and global application development.
