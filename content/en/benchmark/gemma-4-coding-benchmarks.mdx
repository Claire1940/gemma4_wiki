## Hardware Requirements for Local Execution

One of the most impressive aspects of the **gemma 4 coding benchmarks** is the ability to run these models on consumer-grade hardware. For developers working in 2026, the NVIDIA RTX 4090 remains the gold standard for local AI development due to its 24GB of VRAM.

| Model Size | Minimum VRAM | Recommended GPU | Performance Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gemma 4 9B** | 8GB | RTX 4060 Ti | Good for simple scripts and snippets. |
| **Gemma 4 26B** | 20GB | **RTX 4090 / 5080** | The "sweet spot" for full-stack dev. |
| **Gemma 4 31B** | 24GB | RTX 4090 (Quantized) | Best for complex architectural logic. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Running the 31B model at full precision may exceed 24GB VRAM. It is recommended to use 4-bit or 8-bit quantization (GGUF/EXL2) to maintain high speeds on local workstations.
